Choices for generating textCan use estimate of next word probabilityin various ways to generate text:•Greedychoose most probable term: w*= argmaxtP(wn=t | wn-k ,…,wn-1)•Random samplingsample term according to probabilities:  w*~ P(wn| wn-k ,…,wn-1)•Top-ksamplinglimit sampling to k most likely terms: w* ~ P(wn| wn-k ,…,wn-1)1(wn∈top-k)•Temperature sampling limit sampling to likely terms by raising probabilities to power w* ~ P(wn|wn-k ,…,wn-1)1/T where T is the temperature (higher temperature = more uniform sampling)•Beam search search forward one step at a time for most likely sequence (wn,wn+1,wn+2,wn+3,wn+4,…)while limiting the search space to a maximum set of kcandidate sequencesGreedy techniques always produce exactly same text, while sampling produces different text each time

What is Perplexity?
Perplexity of language model quantiﬁes level of surprise/confusionat seeing new text§measures how unlikely theobserved data isunder the modelCalculating perplexity: 1.compute probability of observed sequence P(w1,w2,…wn)under model •for a bigram model have: P(w1,w2,…wn)= P(w1)P(w2|w1)P(w3|w2)… P(wn|wn-1)2.normalize probability for length of text sequence•i.e.compute “average” per-word probability P(w1,w2,…wn)1/n3.invert probability to compute uncertainty: PP(w)= 1/P(w1,w2,…wn)1/n= P(w1,w2,…wn)-1/n
Minimizing perplexity same as maximizing probability§so lower perplexity= better model
